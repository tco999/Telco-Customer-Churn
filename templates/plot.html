On this dataset, we have 7043 customers with 26,5% Churn Rate

Categorical Features



Analysis
From all the features, i categorized them based on the Churn rate. These are the features that has the higher Churn rate than the rest of the features (around 40% or more):
* Internet service – fiber optic: 42% churn rate
* Internet service – no online security: 42% churn rate
* Internet service – no online backup: 40% churn rate
* Internet service – no device protection: 39% churn rate
* Internet service – no tech support: 42% churn rate
* Contract – month-to-month: 43% churn rate
* Payment method – electronic check: 45% churn rate
* Shorter tenure period -> higher churn rate
* Higher Monthly charges -> higher churn rate


Numerical Features





Analysis
* We can infer that the customers that has relatively low Tenure tends to Churn (Shorter Tenure period customers has higher Churn rate than longer ones).
* The longer our customers use our service, the Churn rate will be lower.
* We can infer that customers with higher MonthlyCharges (in the range of around 70 until 110) tends to Churn.
* From the scatterplot, we can see the pattern of our churned customers; it is relatively dense in the area of short Tenure and high MonthlyCharges.
